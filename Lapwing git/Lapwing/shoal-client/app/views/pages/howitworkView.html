How it works

Silverwing is built from a simple concept:

     1) The more each product is bought, the further the price drops.

     2) If the price drops after you buy it, you'll be credited the difference.

Let's use the following example to show this in action.

Meet Amanda and Bill, they both want to buy laptops for their businesses.



Amanda logs in to Silverwing and decides which laptop she wants, pays the current price (£500) and has the laptop delivered to her the next day.



After this, Bill logs on and can see an updated price curve of the laptop that Amanda bought. He can see that one laptop has been bought. If he purchases the same laptop, he would only pay £450 as two units total have been sold.

Bill buys this laptop, pays £450 and has his laptop delivered the next day.



As the current price has now dropped to £450, Amanda receives the difference of £50 in Silverwing Credits.

£500 (Price Amanda Paid) - £450 (Current Price) = £50 (Silverwing Credits for Amanda)

Amanda can see £50 of Silverwing Credits in her account. She can either use these Silverwing Credits towards future purchases on Silverwing or she can withdraw them into her bank account.



This price curve would reach it's target price of £400 if 3 units total are sold. Amanda would then have a total of £100 in Silverwing Credits and Bill would have £50 in Silverwing Credits. At the end of the offer period, everyone who bought this laptop will have paid the same net amount.
